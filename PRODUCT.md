product

## Users

Catholic Fasting serves Catholics who want a clear, pastoral answer about fasting, abstinence, Friday penance, and seasonal discipline without digging through calendars or church documents during an already busy day. The product covers iPhone, iPad, Mac, and widgets, with local-only storage and guidance that supports Canada and U.S. profiles.

Primary users are lay Catholics planning personal observance, households preparing for upcoming required days, and premium users who want steadier formation through Lent, Advent, Ordinary Time, and other seasonal rhythms. Their context is often practical and time-bound: checking today's obligation, planning meals, tracking a fast, reviewing a missed day, or confirming the rationale behind a rule.

## Product Purpose

The app helps users understand what the Church asks today, plan ahead for fasting and abstinence days, and build a steady rhythm of personal discipline through the liturgical year. It combines daily guidance, planning calendars, rule transparency, citations, intermittent fast tracking, reminders, premium formation tools, local exports, and privacy-first storage.

Success means a user can open the app and quickly answer "What should I do today?", understand why, and act with peace rather than confusion. Premium success means the app becomes a calm companion for preparation, recovery, reflection, and seasonal growth rather than a louder productivity layer.

## Brand Personality

The voice is calm, reverent, practical, and transparent. It should feel like a trusted parish handout refined into a native Apple app: clear enough for quick decisions, warm enough for devotional use, and careful enough around pastoral nuance.

Three-word personality: calm, reverent, practical.

## Anti-references

- Do not make the app feel like a generic wellness, fasting, diet, biohacking, or productivity tracker.
- Do not use energetic streak pressure, shame-heavy completion language, aggressive gamification, or copy that implies spiritual performance.
- Do not obscure rule uncertainty, local variation, medical prudence, or pastoral authority behind oversimplified answers.
- Do not turn sacred imagery into decoration for decoration's sake.
- Do not introduce broad visual rewrites that make the Mac app less native or the iOS app less readable.
- Do not add analytics, account sync, or cloud assumptions to a local-only privacy posture.

## Design Principles

1. Daily clarity before ornament. The first job of every surface is to answer the user's immediate obligation or next action.
2. Reverence through restraint. Sacred imagery, seasonal color, and serif moments should support prayerful attention, not compete with the task.
3. Transparent authority. Rules, citations, regional profiles, and rationale should remain close enough that users can inspect the source of guidance.
4. Platform-native calm. iPhone, iPad, Mac, and widgets should respect their native navigation, control density, accessibility, and interaction patterns.
5. Formation without pressure. Tracking, reminders, premium tools, and recovery flows should encourage steady discipline without shame or performative metrics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The product should meet or exceed platform accessibility expectations for iOS, iPadOS, macOS, and widgets. Preserve stable accessibility identifiers where tests depend on them, support Dynamic Type and readable text wrapping, avoid color-only meaning, and keep tap targets large enough for repeated daily use.

Motion should remain subtle and purposeful, with system reduced-motion behavior respected. Copy should support English, Spanish, and French Canadian localization, avoid idioms that are hard to translate, and stay clear about medical prudence, pastoral advice, and local diocesan authority.
