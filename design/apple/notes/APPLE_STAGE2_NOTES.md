## Practical takeaways for Catholic Fasting 4.0
- Keep serif usage narrow: observance, seasonal, and premium-journey emphasis only.
- Default controls should stay native and calm: compact date pickers, standard menu pickers, rounded button hierarchy.
- iPad workspace design should favor one clear primary lane and one quieter support lane.
- Touch targets and supporting text should bias toward readability over density.
- Materials and elevated surfaces should support content hierarchy, not compete with it.
- Accessibility remains a design rule, not a retrofit: larger text, contrast, and calmer grouping matter across Today, Track Fast, and Premium.
- The new design language is most useful to us as a constraint on hierarchy and spacing, not as a reason to over-glass the UI.
